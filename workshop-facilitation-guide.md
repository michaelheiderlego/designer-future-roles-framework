# Workshop Facilitation Guide
## "What Do We Want Our Jobs to Be?" - Designer Future Roles Workshop

*A structured conversation toolkit for design leadership*

---

## Workshop Overview

**Purpose:** Help the design team actively shape their future roles in an AI-augmented environment, rather than passively reacting to change.

**Core Question:** What do we WANT our jobs to be?

**Duration Options:**
- **Express:** 90 minutes (leadership team alignment)
- **Standard:** 3 hours (full design team)
- **Deep Dive:** Full day (team + stakeholders)

**Materials Needed:**
- "The Designer as Director" framework poster (printed large or projected)
- Designer Toolkit guide (printed or digital)
- Sticky notes (3 colors)
- Markers
- Whiteboard or large wall space
- Optional: Miro board for remote facilitation

---

## Pre-Workshop Preparation

### 1 Week Before

**Send to participants:**
- Calendar invite with clear purpose
- Pre-read: Framework poster + 1-page context
- Pre-work exercise (15 min):
  - "Think of a recent project where your designer judgment made the crucial difference. What would have happened if AI had done that part alone?"

**Facilitator prep:**
- Review the toolkit thoroughly
- Identify 2-3 recent team projects to use as examples
- Prepare specific AI tools the team might use
- Consider team dynamics and potential resistance points

### 2 Days Before

**Logistics check:**
- Print materials (1 poster per 4 people)
- Prepare sticky notes and markers
- Test any tech/screen sharing
- Arrange room for small groups

## Workshop Agenda: Standard 3-Hour Version

### Part 1: Grounding (30 min)

**Opening (10 min)**
- Welcome and purpose
- Share the central question: "What do we WANT our jobs to be?"
- Set norms: Honesty, curiosity, no judgment, future-focused

**Individual Reflection (10 min)**
Silent writing exercise with prompts:
- What parts of your current job energize you most?
- What tasks feel like they drain time from more valuable work?
- If AI could take 3 tasks off your plate tomorrow, which would you choose?
- What uniquely human skill do you most want to develop?

**Pair Share (10 min)**
Partners share their reflections, identify common themes

---

### Part 2: Explore the Framework (45 min)

**Introduce the Model (15 min)**
Walk through "The Designer as Director" framework:
- The three concentric zones
- The five human checkpoints
- The AI-augmented outer ring

**Small Group Exercise: Map Current Reality (30 min)**
Groups of 3-4, each with a poster

**Task:** Using sticky notes, map where your team currently spends time:
- **Red notes:** Human core work (judgment, vision, saying no)
- **Orange notes:** Checkpoint work (directing, reviewing, deciding)
- **Blue notes:** AI-augmentable work (execution, variation, synthesis)

**Prompts:**
- Think about last quarter's projects
- Where do you spend MOST time? (large notes)
- Where do you spend LEAST time? (small notes)
- Where do you WISH you spent time? (star these)

**Share Out:** Each group shares their current reality map (2 min each)

---

### Part 3: Confront the Tensions (30 min)

**Fishbowl Discussion**
4-5 participants in center, others observe (rotate halfway through)

**Discussion prompts:**
1. **Fear:** "What's your honest fear about AI and design roles?"
   - Job security
   - Loss of craft
   - Becoming commoditized
   - Design becoming undervalued

2. **Opportunity:** "What excites you about designing with AI?"
   - Faster iteration
   - More time for strategy
   - Ability to test more ideas
   - Focus on judgment over execution

3. **Power dynamics:** "How might the product trio relationship change?"
   - When PMs can prototype
   - When engineers make design choices
   - How do we maintain influence?

**Facilitator role:**
- Hold space for real concerns
- Don't minimize fears
- Help group distinguish between inevitable changes and choices we can make
- Bridge to next section: "We can't control all changes, but we can choose how we respond"

---

### Part 4: Define Desired Future (60 min)

**Return to Small Groups**

**Exercise 1: Non-Negotiables (20 min)**
"What must remain designer-led, regardless of AI capabilities?"

Each group creates list of non-negotiables:
- Skills that must stay human
- Decision points that require designer judgment
- Quality standards that can't be automated
- Values that define good design work

**Share and Consolidate (10 min)**
Combine into master list, vote on top 5 team non-negotiables

---

**Exercise 2: Checkpoint Customization (30 min)**
"How do we operationalize human-in-the-loop in OUR context?"

Groups tackle one checkpoint each:
1. Problem definition & scope
2. Concept direction & principles
3. Quality judgment & refinement
4. Ethical/human impact review
5. Simplicity edit

**For your assigned checkpoint, define:**
- What triggers this checkpoint? (When does designer intervene?)
- What specific questions do we ask?
- What do we look for in review?
- What's our quality bar?
- How do we document our decisions?
- What tools/templates would help?

**Gallery Walk:**
Groups post their checkpoint definitions, everyone reviews and adds comments

---

### Part 5: Skill Development Plan (30 min)

**Individual Exercise (15 min)**
Each person completes:

**My Core Skills Audit:**
Rate yourself (1-5) on the five irreplaceable core skills:
- [ ] Moral compass & judgment
- [ ] Envisioning desired futures
- [ ] Creating simplicity from chaos
- [ ] Bridge to human experience
- [ ] Courage to say "no"

**My Development Focus:**
- Which core skill do I most want to strengthen?
- What's one concrete action I can take in the next month?
- What support do I need from the team/leadership?

**Small Group Huddle (15 min)**
Groups of 3-4 share:
- Development focuses
- Create peer accountability pairs
- Identify shared learning opportunities

---

### Part 6: Practical Next Steps (15 min)

**Whole Group - Commitments**

**As individuals, we will:**
- [ ] Pick one AI tool to experiment with this month
- [ ] Practice one core skill deliberately
- [ ] Share learnings in [team channel/meeting]

**As a team, we will:**
- [ ] Adopt the checkpoint framework for [specific project type]
- [ ] Create toolkit templates by [date]
- [ ] Schedule monthly AI learning share
- [ ] Review and update our approach quarterly

**Leadership will:**
- [ ] Allocate time for skill development
- [ ] Support tool experimentation
- [ ] Protect designer decision rights at checkpoints
- [ ] Champion designer value with stakeholders

**Closing Round (10 min)**
Popcorn style - each person shares one word about how they're feeling leaving this session

## Alternative Formats

### 90-Minute Express Version (Leadership Team)

**Purpose:** Align leadership on approach before rolling out to full team

**Agenda:**
1. **Grounding (10 min):** Shared context, why this matters
2. **Framework Introduction (15 min):** Walk through model
3. **Strategic Discussion (40 min):**
   - What are our biggest risks if we don't address this?
   - What opportunities do we see?
   - What's our point of view as leadership?
   - How do we communicate this to the team?
4. **Team Rollout Planning (20 min):**
   - Who should be involved?
   - When should we run full workshop?
   - What questions will the team have?
   - What commitments can we make?
5. **Next Steps (5 min)**

---

### Full-Day Deep Dive (Team + Stakeholders)

**Purpose:** Align design team AND product trio partners on new ways of working

**Additional elements:**
- Invite PM and engineering leads
- **Morning:** Run standard workshop with design team
- **Lunch:** Informal mixing
- **Afternoon (2.5 hours):**
  - Present framework to stakeholders
  - Cross-functional discussion: How does this change our collaboration?
  - Define trio working agreements
  - Create pilot project plan
  - Establish review cadence

## Facilitation Tips

### Creating Psychological Safety

**Do:**
- Acknowledge fears openly
- Share your own uncertainties
- Use "we" language, not "you"
- Celebrate honesty
- Frame as exploration, not mandate

**Don't:**
- Minimize concerns ("Don't worry, you'll be fine")
- Rush past discomfort
- Enforce false positivity
- Present as already decided
- Speak for the team

---

### Managing Different Reactions

**The Enthusiast** ("This is amazing!")
- Harness energy
- Ask to share examples
- Channel into helping skeptics

**The Skeptic** ("This will never work")
- Validate concerns
- Ask: "What would need to be true for this to work?"
- Find small experiments they'd try

**The Worried** ("Will I still have a job?")
- Address directly
- Connect to valued skills
- Show path forward

**The Pragmatist** ("What's the actual plan?")
- Appreciate groundedness
- Co-create next steps
- Give ownership of implementation

**The Quiet Observer**
- Make space in pairs/small groups
- Use written exercises
- Check in 1:1 afterward

---

### Handling Tough Questions

**"Will we have fewer designers?"**
*"That's a fair question. What I know is that design will remain critical, but what we DO may shift. This workshop is about ensuring we're doing high-value work, not just more work. Our goal is to shape that shift, not let it happen to us."*

**"Isn't this just making designers obsolete?"**
*"I understand why it feels that way. But think about past technology shifts - when designers moved from print to digital, the medium changed but the need for design judgment grew. AI is a tool, like Sketch or Figma. The question is: what becomes MORE important about our role?"*

**"What if stakeholders start using AI to bypass designers?"**
*"That's exactly why we need to proactively define our value. If we're clear about where designer judgment is essential and can demonstrate that impact, we have a strong position. Plus, we can teach stakeholders to use AI BETTER."*

**"This feels like we're planning our own replacement."**
*"I hear you. It can feel that way. But consider the alternative - if we DON'T engage with this shift, others will decide for us. This is us taking agency. We're not planning our replacement, we're planning our evolution."*

## Measuring Workshop Success

### Immediate (End of workshop)
- Participants can explain the three-zone model
- Everyone has identified one skill to develop
- Team has defined at least 3 non-negotiables
- Clear next steps documented

### 1 Month Later
- At least 50% of team has tried one AI tool
- Checkpoint model piloted on at least one project
- Regular learning share happening
- Visible enthusiasm/engagement

### 3 Months Later
- Checkpoint process integrated into standard workflow
- Measurable shift in time allocation (less execution, more judgment)
- Shared language established ("core vs. checkpoint vs. augmented")
- Stakeholders recognize designer value proposition

### 6 Months Later
- Product trio collaboration model evolved
- Designers report spending more time on strategic work
- Toolkit actively used and updated
- Team confident in their unique value

## Troubleshooting Common Challenges

### Challenge: "We don't have time for skill development"
**Response:** Frame as investment, not cost
- Show how AI-augmented execution frees time
- Start with 30-min weekly learning slots
- Make it project-embedded, not separate
- Track time saved by AI to fund development

### Challenge: "Stakeholders don't value designer judgment"
**Response:** Make value visible
- Document decisions and impact
- Show before/after of checkpoint interventions
- Quantify avoided mistakes
- Educate stakeholders on checkpoints

### Challenge: "Different designers have very different comfort with AI"
**Response:** Meet people where they are
- Create learning buddies (high/low comfort pairs)
- Share small wins publicly
- Provide range of starting points
- Emphasize human skills for AI-hesitant folks

### Challenge: "This is moving too fast/too slow"
**Response:** Adjust pace
- Too fast: More workshops, smaller experiments
- Too slow: Increase urgency, show external examples
- Create fast track and thoughtful track options

## Customization Guidance

**Adapt this workshop for your context:**

**If your team is:**
- **Early career:** Add more skill-building workshops after
- **Senior:** Focus more on leadership/influence aspects
- **Skeptical of AI:** Lead with opportunities, acknowledge fears early
- **Enthusiastic about AI:** Focus on quality standards and judgment
- **Remote:** Use Miro heavily, add more breaks, shorten to 2.5 hours
- **Co-located:** Use physical materials, encourage movement

**If your organization:**
- **Is risk-averse:** Emphasize checkpoints and quality gates
- **Moves fast:** Focus on rapid experimentation framework
- **Values craft:** Highlight how AI enables better craft through iteration
- **Is data-driven:** Include metrics and measurement throughout

## Facilitator Self-Reflection

**Before you facilitate, ask yourself:**
- What's MY relationship with AI and design?
- What fears am I bringing to this?
- What opportunities excite me?
- Can I hold space for views different from mine?
- Am I ready to not have all the answers?

**Remember:**
- You're facilitating discovery, not providing answers
- The team's insights will be richer than your plan
- Your role is to create structure for their thinking
- It's okay to say "I don't know, let's figure it out together"

**After the workshop:**
- What surprised you?
- What energy did you notice?
- What needs more attention?
- How are you personally growing through this?

---

## Final Thoughts for Facilitators

This workshop is not about getting everyone excited about AI. It's about:
- **Agency:** Designers actively shaping their future
- **Clarity:** Defining what's essential vs. what's delegable
- **Skill:** Developing capabilities that remain valuable
- **Community:** Supporting each other through change

Some people will leave energized. Others will leave thoughtful or even uncertain. Both are good outcomes. What matters is that people are ENGAGED with the question, not passive to the change.

The goal isn't consensus, it's conversation. You're launching a multi-month (or multi-year) dialogue, not concluding one.

**Trust the process. Trust your team. Trust that designers who've navigated every previous technology shift will navigate this one too.**
